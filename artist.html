Katushika Hokusair

Katsushika Hokusai was born on October 31, 1760, and died on May 10th, 1849 at the young age of 88. Hokusai began painting around the age of six and they thing he learned from his father. During his life Hokusai was sent to work at a library at the age of 12 by his father, after being there for a year he became a pupil and his master gave him the name of Shunro and under this name he published his first prints and a series of pictures of Kabuki actors that was published in 1779. Years later he married his first wife and very little was known about her except that she passed away in the early 1790s and 7 years later he remarried but unfortunately his second wife passed away shortly afterward. He is the father of two sons and three daughters with the two wives and his youngest daughter Ei, also known as Oi, became an artist as well. Later in his life, Hokusai developed many brush paintings called surimono, and illustrations for Kyoka ehon (humorous poems).
